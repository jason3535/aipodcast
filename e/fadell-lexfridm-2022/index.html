托尼·法德尔分享了他通过打孔卡和 BASIC 编程对计算机的初次热爱，以及电子艺界的构建工具如何激发他的创造力。

Tony Fadell shares his first love for computers through punch cards and BASIC, and how Electronic Arts' construction sets inspired him to create.

核心观点 · Key points

  • 伟大的创意始于止痛药而非维生素，并带来情感上的超能力。
    Great ideas start with a painkiller, not a vitamin, and deliver an emotional superpower.
  • 对于初代产品，决策基于观点而非数据；领导者必须为此负责。
    For v1 products, decisions are opinion-based, not data-driven; leaders must own them.
  • 从一份新闻稿开始，作为剧本让团队对齐目标和故事。
    Start with a press release as a treatment to align the team on the why and story.
  • 产品管理是客户的声音，最难招聘，并让团队保持脚踏实地。
    Product management is the voice of the customer, hardest to hire for, and keeps the team grounded.
  • 沟通中的真实性和冒险精神至关重要；领导者定下基调。
    Authenticity and risk-taking in communication are essential; leaders set the tone.

反共识 · Contrarian takes

  • 专家可能对创新有害；初学者心态更适合解决新问题。
    Experts can be detrimental to innovation; beginners' mindset is better for novel problems.
  • 史蒂夫·乔布斯是可以被挑战的；他批评工作而非人，并可能被数据说服。
    Steve Jobs was challengeable; he critiqued work, not people, and could be swayed by data.
  • iPhone最初选择塑料；乔布斯通过将划痕归咎于客户而转向玻璃。
    Plastic on iPhone was initially chosen; Jobs flipped the frame to glass by blaming customer for scratches.
  • 营销应从开始就参与，而不仅仅是在最后讲故事。
    Marketing should be involved from the start, not just at the end to tell a story.
  • iPod成功是因为苹果将所有营销资金投入其中，尽管当时公司处境艰难。
    The iPod succeeded because Apple committed all marketing dollars to it, despite being a struggling company.
